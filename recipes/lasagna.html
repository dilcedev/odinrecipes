Lasagna

Home Page

Lasagna is a beloved Italian dish known for its layers of rich flavors and comforting texture. Its foundation consists of pasta sheets, cheese, meat, and a savory tomato-based sauce, all baked together to create a mouthwatering combination. Whether you prefer a classic meat lasagna or a vegetarian version, it never fails to deliver warmth and satisfaction.

Ingredients:

  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 1 egg
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil or parsley (optional, for garnish)

Here's how you make it:

  1. Cook the noodles: Boil the lasagna noodles according to package instructions, drain, and set aside.
  2. Prepare the meat sauce: In a skillet, brown the ground beef over medium heat. Add the marinara sauce, salt, and pepper, and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
  3. Make the cheese mixture: In a bowl, combine ricotta cheese, mozzarella, Parmesan, egg, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Mix well.
  4. Layer the lasagna: Preheat the oven to 375°F (190°C). In a baking dish, spread a thin layer of meat sauce, then add a layer of noodles. Top with cheese mixture and repeat the layers, finishing with a layer of sauce and mozzarella on top.
  5. Bake: Cover with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes until the cheese is bubbly and golden.
  6. Serve: Let it cool for a few minutes before serving. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired.
